Mehtab Bagh

History

Mehtab Bagh is a charbagh-style Mughal garden located on the northern bank of the Yamuna River, directly opposite the Taj Mahal. It was built by Emperor Babur and later restored by the Archaeological Survey of India to provide a serene view of the Taj Mahal.

  • Founder: Emperor Babur
  • Period: Early Mughal era
  • Purpose: Moonlight garden and viewing point
  • Location: Opposite Taj Mahal across Yamuna

What to Explore?

  1. Taj Mahal View: One of the best sunset views of the Taj.
  2. Charbagh Layout: Traditional Mughal four-part garden.
  3. Reflection Pools: Symmetry aligned with the Taj.
  4. Photography: Ideal spot for peaceful photos.
  5. Yamuna Riverfront: Calm riverside atmosphere.

Tips for Exploration

  • Visit during sunset for best views.
  • Carry a camera for photography.
  • Evening hours are cooler and pleasant.
