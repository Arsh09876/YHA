Red Fort Agra

History

The Red Fort of Agra, built by Mughal Emperor Akbar in the 16th century, served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors and a symbol of imperial power. Constructed primarily with red sandstone, it features Persian, Islamic, and Hindu architectural influences.

What to Explore?

  1. Diwan-i-Aam: Hall of Public Audience where the emperor addressed the people.
  2. Diwan-i-Khas: Hall of Private Audience with ornate decorations.
  3. Moti Masjid: Pearl Mosque inside the fort built by Shah Jahan.
  4. Anguri Bagh: Mughal-style garden with geometric flowerbeds.
  5. Sheesh Mahal: Mirror Palace adorned with decorative mirrors.
  6. Machchhi Bhawan: Guest house for visiting dignitaries.
  7. View of Yamuna River: Offers panoramic views of the river and fort exterior.

Tips for Exploration

  • Open daily except Fridays.
  • Security checks are strict; avoid large bags.
  • Wear comfortable shoes for walking the extensive fort area.
