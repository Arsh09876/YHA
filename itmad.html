History

Itmad-ul-Daula‚Äôs Tomb, often called the ‚ÄúBaby Taj‚Äù, was built between 1622 and 1628 by Nur Jahan in memory of her father, Mirza Ghiyas Beg (Itmad-ul-Daula). It is considered a precursor to the Taj Mahal and marks a major transition from red sandstone to white marble Mughal architecture.

  • Purpose: Tomb of Mirza Ghiyas Beg.
  • Construction: Built during the reign of Emperor Jahangir.
  • Architectural Style: Early Mughal marble architecture.
  • Materials: White marble with pietra dura inlay.
  • Legacy: First Mughal tomb built entirely of marble.

What to Explore?

  1. Marble Mausoleum: Delicate white marble structure with lattice windows.
  2. Pietra Dura Work: Floral inlays using semi-precious stones.
  3. Charbagh Garden: Symmetrical Mughal garden layout.
  4. Corner Minarets: Decorative towers adding elegance.
  5. Yamuna River View: Peaceful river-side setting.
  6. Intricate Carvings: Fine geometric and floral designs.

Tips for Exploration

  • Best visited in morning or late afternoon.
  • Photography allowed.
  • Less crowded than the Taj Mahal.
